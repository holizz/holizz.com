Usability On The Web

According to the Wikipedia, usability is the measure of how easily a thing can be used.

Why?

Why is usability important? Because if people can’t use your site they leave, quite simply.

How?

One of the most common and useful methods of achieving usability is by testing the site.

First, you get a load of willing volunteers. Next you put them in front of a copy of your Web site, whether it be on paper, or on a computer. Then they’re asked to identify certain parts of the site like the navigation, title, etc. or navigate the site or find something.

Accessibility

A11y is the usability of a system by everybody, especially those with disabilities and people using alternative software/hardware, etc..

About 10% of American males have some colour-perception difficulty, for example. Thus low-contrast colours should not need to be distinguishable.

One common problem in a11y is Web authors using img elements without the alt attribute even though it’s been required since HTML 4. But then Web authors tend to write tag soup anyway.

Some examples

Here’s the IWT Web site. It has red text on a green background. Thus it’s probably not too readable to 1 in 10 people with red-green colour-blindness. It’s also kind of garish and I have at least one friend who refuses to read it because of the colour combination.
