FQ: Foreign

Bit late, as usual. But look at me using all those ISO country/language codes!

FQ1: What’s your native language? Do you speak any foreign languages? If so, how did you come to learn them?

My first language is English (en-GB). I learnt French and German a bit but I forgot it all.

My second language is BSL (sgn-GB). I learnt it at Bradford College about two years ago and I still remember a fair bit. I’d been wanting to learn it for much longer though. It’s also the reason I am careful when I use the term foreign language because it is neither my first language nor is it foreign.

My third language is Esperanto (eo), which is also not a foreign language, since it is an international language and not of or belonging to any region.

I expect Italian (it) shall be my fourth language. I shall be learning it over the Summer vacation at the same time as John, then we’ll come back and talk to each other in Italian.

FQ2: What’s your native country? Have you visited any foreign countries? If so, which ones?
Born in England (GB) and I have lived here for all 19 of my years. I have visited: Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland (all GB), Ireland (IE), France (FR), Germany (DE), United States of America (US).
FQ3: Are there any foreign foods, books, movies, or other items that you are particularly fond of? Name some of your favorites.

As a resident of Bradford (damn, no region-code for that one) I am treated to excellent availability of central/southern Asian (I need to research what to call that part of Asia) cuisine and I am rather fond of it. I enjoy eastern Asian cuisine too. That’s where tofu comes from. Did you know that the W3C got me into natto?

As far as books go, I’ve read Sophie’s World (the English translation).

I am fond of many foreign films, I’ve seen a lot. I’m not sure much beats Amélie or End of Evangelion.

I also like gender-neutral pronouns. Such as ri in Riismo in Esperanto or hän/se in Finnish.

FQ NATIVE: If you had to trade your nationality for that of any foreign country, which would you choose and why?
Mu. However I can imagine moving to another country semi-permanently. That would be fun. Hell, anything’s fun after you’ve lived in Bradford 19 years.
