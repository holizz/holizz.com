South Square Centre’s poor choices

I was almost crying. They changed the URIs.

For a little context: Guess what I was today!

Look at the first page of a Google for site:southsquarecentre.co.uk. Most of Google’s links are to non-existant pages.

So my URI-naming scheme wasn’t perfect, at least directories with content didn’t just give a listing. And a space in a directory name? Whose stupid-headed idea was that? And all the files are something.htm. Gah, gah, gah! How can they do this? I feel like a part of me has been destroyed. So much effort, all gone!

And that’s just the URIs! Also of note is that the new pages are invalid HTML with no DOCTYPE, even. They have images for the navigation text when there is no reason whatsoever to do this—they don’t even have the excuse of them being “rollovers”. The image-links don’t have alt text either, which is really bloody useful. They’re using tables for layout too. And the pages are very bloated, leading me to assume they were created with some inaccessible-shit–making machine like Dreamweaver. Also, the logo has been shrunk to fit the navigation bar, not by cropping it or even resizing it. Do you know how they did it? They set its height in HTML. Why? It just looks like a badly–scaled-down image.

Enough of the negative: I like the new layout. It’s nice.

Enough of the positive: They destroyed something I created! It was good, and accessible. I even did a very small-scale usability study! Well. They want inaccessible shite instead of usable, cool-URI, user-tested, valid-XHTML 1.1 goodness? They can have it!
