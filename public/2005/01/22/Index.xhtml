We all know I’m a super-1337 h4x0r. But what have I done today (and yesterday) that’s so 1337?

Yesterday I went to Maplin with my less-1337-than-me friend known as Niall. I purchased a null modem cable. As a result I can connect my laptop to astro (my desktop) and use it as a terminal. It’s so cool. I shall experiment more with it tomorrow (and maybe try and stop minicom from crashing all the time).

Just now I made magic happen with MultiViews and mod_rewrite. Now as you may notice, everything is refered to by a simple name with no horrible .html, for example. This is thanks you MultiViews. I’d been having some trouble getting sunc to rewrite to sunc.cgi. Niall’s great idea was to stick sunc.cgi in a seperate directory. And guess what, it worked! I love you, Niall. Now that I have these nice URIs I shall keep them free from link-rot as far as is humanly possible.

I came across an interesting article. The author brings up some good points. Possibly some things that need to be fixed if we expect anybody but standards geeks to create good Web pages.

I did wonder at ‘valid’ and ‘will be valid forever’. For example, EasySpace’s formatted logs claim to be valid HTML 2.0 (I believe) and they are. They were, are, and always will be valid HTML 2.0. Nothing can change that. Specifications don’t change.

You know you’re a geek when …

By the way, that was me. You can tell because it’s valid XHTML 1.1.

FQ3: What’s a new place you’ve been to or web site you’ve visited? How’d it go?

I discovered this page the other day. It’s exactly what I’ve been looking for. And unlike Serial Terminal Linux, it wasn’t compiled with an Intel-specific kernel and the link to the source hasn’t gone rotten. It appears to work. So it’s off to Maplin today.

I also revisited anticAPSLOCK.com. I took the plunge and used my ~/.Xmodmap to move my caps lock to my left logo key and replaced my caps lock with a control. I then went on to map another escape to the ‘ (grave) key (above tab, left of 1, below escape) to make vimming easier. I use the grave all the time so that’s been moved to the right logo key.

FQ NEW: Do the new! What’s something new you’ve been wanting to try but haven’t? What’s stopping you?

Coding in CLISP. I started it … but I just stopped. I’m sure when I get better at it it will be like a second blogging (in that I get addicted to it, almost). I found some nice tutorials though so I may do some more today.

I also kind of want a Happy Hacker keyboard. The only problems are that it’s a US layout (which could be rather hard to get used to) and that it’s £59.00. I could afford it, of course, and I could get used to the layout (or xmodmap the hell out of it). It’s just that I might not want to do both when I have a working keyboard already.
