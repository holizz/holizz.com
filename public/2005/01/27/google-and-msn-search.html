Google and MSN Search

I found an interesting report on the relative rankings of Apache and IIS web servers on Google and MSN (Beta) Search.

I did a little investigating: Google for “holizz”, MSN Search for “holizz”.

On Google, the first result is my old blog. Fair enough as it’s been there for a while. The second is another result from my old blog. Next it’s my old URI (this is why it’s so important never to change URIs), which redirects to the next result, which is my new URI. So my current blog is number three on Google.

MSN Search is another matter altogether. The first result is a comment on Slashdot. The second result is a comment on Slashdot. Then it’s my tag on a Wikipedia talk page. Then Slashdot. Heh, my BME personal, but it’s a link to a result page so it’s outof date and I’m no longer there. Then a search result on Bloglines for “beautiful browser”. Then a result from some Asian community board, which is just somebody trying to spell “holiday” very, very badly. Then Slashdot. Then “Domains that have linked to neilturner.me.uk in the last 10 days”, which includes a link to my old Blogger blog. End of first page.

Well. Not a single link to either my old blog or my current blog. How rude of MSN Beta Search. Not a single result for my blogs on normal MSN Search either.

And just for fun I Googled for “Tom Adams” (that’s my name, don’t wear it out). I’m number 53. It’s not my blog or anything sensible like that, it’s my report on usability. I was thinking 2004-2005 was a bad URI. But I suppose it’s not so bad. And I can’t change it now that it’s number 53 on Google. Well, I suppose it’ll disappear at some point due to me leaving university, but ah well. That’s referring to the school year 2004–2005, by the way.
