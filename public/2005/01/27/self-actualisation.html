Self-Actualisation

I got onto thinking about self-actualisation today. Rather than spend many hours pondering (thus taking away precious time from my latest project which will be unveiled shortly), I thought I’d do a quiz.

For those non-geeks: T means true, NIL means false and then there’s Mu, which means the question makes no sense or doesn’t apply to me.

  1. Overall, I make growth choices as opposed to fear choices. T.
  2. I listen to my own voice, discover my own preferences and adhere to them. T.
  3. I am honest, don’t play games and am self-responsible. T.
  4. I dare to be different: a non-conformist, unpopular and socially courageous when need be. Not sure about socially courageous. But I try. Unpopular? Me? <span class="sarcasm">I think I think I may just about fit into the non-conformist category.</span> Uh, I mean, I won’t conform to your fascist categories! (Phew, I almost lost my credibility as a non-conformist there ;). T.
  5. I am willing to arduously prepare, do the thing I want to do well and be as good as I can be. I’m not sure I’ve ever had to arduously prepare for anything I wanted to do. Mu/T (actual answer: Mu, answer for statistics: T).
  6. I am a good judge and can easily detect falseness in things and people. This would be a good case for interviews rather than self-completion questionnaires: I’m not sure what the question is asking. Mu and since I doubt I’d be good at detecting falseness in people: /NIL.
  7. I am open-minded and withhold judgement of the unknown. I’d say T for the most part. I have been known to jump to conclusions (think UML).
  8. I appreciate instinctual pleasures in a balanced, uninhibited way. I’d say T. I like eating and exercise although I don’t get enough of either. I dislike masturbation. Pretty balanced.
  9. I love, honour and respect myself. Of course I do. How could I not love the best hacker in Bradford, possibly even Yorkshire? I just Googled for hacker yorkshire and found no hackers living in Yorkshire on the first page. Therefore I am the only hacker in Yorkshire. And yes, I do love the only hacker in Yorkshire. (Hehe). T.
  10. I have a wide range of interests and tastes. I must admit that computing is where the vast majority of my attention and time goes. I like other things. But they’re just not as important. NIL.
  11. I have a mission or goal which gets a lot of my time and energy. T. My mission is, ultimately, to become a great hacker (specialising in the Web, of course). It’s entirely possible that I could fail at this goal but I see no reason why considering my estimated life carries on for around another sixty years. I’ve learnt this much in 20 years, I’m sure I can become a great hacker.
  12. I am concerned with philosophical questions. Not particularly. NIL.
  13. I use privacy for constructive solitude. T.
  14. I am teachable and eager to learn from all sources. As I say, I love nothing more than learning. T.
  15. I have strong feelings for humanity/community consciousness. In what sense? I think Gaia, as implemented in the Foundation series, would be a fantastic thing. Mu/T.
  16. I am creative, original and inventive. I’d certainly say so. Although at the moment I’ve been stuck in a loop. I’ve written two CMSes already this year and I wrote one just before the new year and I wrote one a few months back for South Square (by Space, it’s changed, I shall blog about it and post it after this one). T.
  17. I have a small (or large) group of friends interested in personal growth and service. Uh. I’m sure most of my friends love personal growth. But to what extent I could not say. I’ll say T.
  18. I have deep, profound, loving and empathic relationships. I’m not sure I have many relationships like that. NIL.
  19. I am kind and respectful to others. T. I’m always polite and respectful. Except inaccessible Web designers. I send them hate mail. But after reading a recent post on Dooce, I realise it may not be the best way of going about things. Stay tuned for polite email I send to the Web masters of Web sites that annoy me to death.
  20. I am able to enjoy and accept process/being in transition. I enjoy it more than I used to, so T.
  21. I believe in the metaphysical concept of “universal good”. I’m not sure I do. I thing Socrates had an idea of Universal good as have many philosophers since, but I think in that respect the pre-Socratic philosophers had it right. Wow. I just applied some knowledge I gained from Sophie’s World NIL.
  22. I experience things fully and vividly, keeping my awareness in the present moment. But of course. T.
  23. I am calm, serene and objective about problems. Most problems. T.
  24. I enjoy being playful. I take life seriously and myself lightly. T.
  25. I typically act instead of react. In what situation? Mu/T.

Then I count my Ts: 20

  • 0–6: Self-actualization is still just a concept. Are you ready to explore it further?
  • 6–11: You’ve made a good beginning. Are you ready to up the ante?
  • 11–16: You’re almost halfway there. What’s next?
  • 16–25: Go for the stretch!

Woop, I rule.
