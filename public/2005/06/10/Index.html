SymphonyOS

Yesterday I went swimming in the morning (well, early in the afternoon). A whole 30 lengths. It made me feel good.

Today I tried out SymphonyOS. It’s certainly a step in the right direction. Usability is something seriously lacking in Linux and open source.

I just have two suggestions: when I’m on a different menu in Mezzo and I click something, I would expect the menu to stay rather than switch back to the main desktop; and there should be a keyboard switching tool somewhere (because I don’t want to have to plug in my PS/2 keyboard at boot and type knoppix26 xkeyboard=dvorak).

But definitely very good. When I convince my mum that she does want Linux on her old computer SymphonyOS will be considered (it’s Symphony or Ubuntu, at the moment). I definitely can’t wait for Mezzo and Orchestra to check into the new testing distribution of Debian.
