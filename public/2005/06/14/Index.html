Archive of 2005-06-14.

Today I woke up

Today I woke up, had breakfast while watching Daria, read my feeds. Then I did 30 lengths in the pool, went to the last book sale of the academic year and got a second book on Italian. Off home with increased circulation and a new book (that book sale will be my financial downfall) for half an hour of meditation and now yummy lunch.

I went swimming for the first time in ages on Thursday mainly because I felt so atrophied having been sitting here most of the year.

On Friday was my last exam, Formal Foundations. I think I did quite well, it was certainly the better of the three. After the exam I was invited to Biko, where all the other post-exam woes would be discussed. But I had forgotten to submit my KSCP5 coursework, so I rushed off to the library thinking I could SSH home, copy it to my user space and print it, but I couldn’t find an SSH client so I had to run home, make one symlink, then go back and print it. I got it in with minutes to spare (note: I did literally run). At some point that day I handed my form in and paid so I can stay in Longside for a couple of weeks. Later that night I went to Rios with Will and John. It was good fun but that’s the last I’ll be seeing of John for a good long while as he left for home in Wales the next day.

Saturday I don’t think I did much.

Sunday I took some boxes of things I shan’t be needing for a while and put them in Davina’s studio (so when I move twice in as many weeks we can do it in one carload each time). I also did some photos for Davina (i.e. sticking kids’ photos in albums which will then be sent to their parents) while watching The Graduate and then Enterprise (which happened to be the last one I watched at Cult TV).

Monday I paid lots of money to the Student Reception.

I have yet to start looking for a Summer job and tell Mr Awan that I would like to move in come the first of July.

In terms of Lisp, I’m still wondering what I’m missing to make all attempts at getting either Portable AllegroServe or Araneida to work fail (yes, I was reading the tutorials). I’ve also had no luck at using any other slightly complex Common Lisp package, like CLIM. I keep getting all sorts of errors about packages not being made, name collisions, and scary things I can’t fix.

Also, I want to be able to compile Common Lisp to ELF (I’d settle for a.out) so I can run Lisp programmes on computers that don’t have Lisp (i.e. my host’s web server).
