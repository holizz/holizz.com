TV, newspaper, book, and Internet usage

  1. On average, Britons spend three hours a day watching TV
  2. Compared to 17 minutes reading newspapers
  3. 11 minutes reading book
  4. And seven minutes online

Source: Self improvement, the couch potato way, via: Spotted: TV, newspaper, book, and internet usage.

How odd.

I only watch actual TV (with the tele- part) if I’m babysitting. I watch 6 hours of TV (without the tele- part—i.e. the visuals come from a DVD and aren’t televised) a week at Cult TV.

I never read newspapers as I can get the exact same news online, not that the news newspapers carry is that interesting anyway.

I tend to have a book I read for an hour or so before I go to sleep (it varies depending on how good it is, how tired I am, etc.) and I’m currently reading a comic (Preacher) and listening to a podcast-only novel (Earth Core). So maybe 2 hours of novels a day.

I tend to watch one or two films a month.

And pretty much every waking moment when I’m at home I am on my computer, which is always connected so I probably spend approximately 12 hours a day online when I have an average amount of social and academic activities.
