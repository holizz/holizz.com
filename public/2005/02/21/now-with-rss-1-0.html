Now with RSS 1.0

So I finally got round to making my RSS 1.0 feed validate.

It’s available here if your user-agent requests application/rss+xml or here if it doesn’t.

It even validates. In fact I had it available for a while before it did. Turns out I just had my item elements inside the channel. It took a good while before I worked it out though because none of the RSS validators told me; they just made obscure complaints.

  • Feed Validator
  • RSS Validator. This one said it was valid even when the other two disagreed.
  • Redland RSS 1.0 Validator and Viewer. This was the most useful because it gave useful information I could use to debug it.

I chose RSS 1.0 (RDF Site Summary in this case) because I quite like RDF at the moment. It troubles me not in the least that there are all these incompatible RSS specifications floating about as I neither use RSS (or any kind of syndication) nor do I write an RSS user agent. I just wanted to increase the ways people can read my site.

It’s generated using XSLT, no less. I apply a simple stylesheet to Index.xhtml.

Next up: Atom. It shouldn’t be too hard, I just have to go off and read the specification and a few examples then I can apply a similar stylesheet.
