Two Nights In A Row

Two nights in a row I have visited the fine establishment known as Rios. It was good fun on Friday. Saturday was a little boring.

Some random guy approached me when I was sitting down as if he wanted to shake my hand. I stood up with my hand ready to shake his and he dragged me onto the dance floor. It was quite odd. And socially it was scary. When I’m with people in unusual situations (or if I don’t know the people I’m with) I generally have to think a lot to try and appear less socially inept than I am. I mainly have to think about issues like “If I do X will I appear to be Y?” where X is any behaviour and Y tends to be something to do with offending them. Because I generally find talking to random strangers difficult I often think along the lines of “how can I appear to like this person and not completely offend them by appearing uninterested in them without talking to them”.

I, Will and Chris walked some person home after Rios; called Emma, I believe. I said to her “I apologise if my sobriety offends you.” at some point and Will thought it was amusing so he told me to blog about it. So I am blogging about it. Hi, Will.

I saw A Very Long Engagement tonight. It was fantastic; I loved it. It was quite sad in parts but it ended well. And the ending wasn’t as clichéd as it could have been.
